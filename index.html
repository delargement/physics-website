Picture a billiard table, with two billiard balls (we’ll label them A and B) placed at the bottom two corners. As you hit both balls towards the middle at the same time, they will rebound into the top two holes. A will go to the left, while B will go to the right. If someone then walks in, even though he didn’t see the collision, he would still know that the ball that that ends up on the left must be A and the one on the right must be B.
